Let a_1, a_2, …, a_n be an integer sequence of length n such
that:

  * a_1 = 6
  * for all 1 ≤ i  n: ϕ(a_i) ϕ(a_i + 1)  a_i  a_i + 1.1

Let S(N) be the number of such sequences with a_n ≤ N.  
For example, S(10) = 4: 6, 6, 8, 6, 8, 9 and 6,
10.  
We can verify that S(100) = 482073668 and S(10 000)  10^8 =
73808307.

Find S(20 000 000)  10^8.

1 ϕ denotes **Euler's totient function**.
