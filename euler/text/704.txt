Define g(n, m) to be the largest integer k such that 2^k divides
nm. For example, 125 = 792 = 2^3 · 3^2 · 11, hence
g(12, 5) = 3. Then define F(n) = max
 g(n, m) : 0 ≤ m ≤ n.
F(10) = 3 and F(100) = 6.

Let S(N) = ∑_n=1^NF(n). You are given that S(100) =
389 and S(10^7) = 203222840.

Find S(10^16).
